Compassion & Comfort

At Impact Link, our journey began with a simple yet profound belief: that every individual facing serious illness deserves to live their final days with dignity, comfort, and surrounded by compassion. Founded in 2015, our organization emerged from a collective desire to ease the burdens faced by patients and their families during challenging times. Our team of dedicated healthcare professionals, including experienced doctors, nurses, counselors, and volunteers, shares a common commitment: to provide holistic and personalized care to our patients. Together, we bring expertise, empathy, and a deep understanding of the unique needs of each individual under our care.


We adopt a patient-centered approach that focuses on managing pain, alleviating symptoms, and improving the overall well-being of our patients. We work closely with patients and their families to create customized care plans that honor their wishes and values. Being a volunteer with Impact link has been a profoundly humbling experience. I've witnessed the power of human connection and the difference small acts of kindness can make during the most challenging times.

Our Projects

Compassionate Caregiver Support Program

Create a program that offers support and resources to family caregivers who are taking care of loved ones receiving palliative care. Caregivers often face physical, emotional, and psychological challenges, and this project aims to provide them with the tools and assistance they need. Reduce caregiver burnout, enhance their ability to provide quality care, and improve the overall well-being of both patients and caregivers. Develop a series of support groups, workshops, and counseling services specifically designed for caregivers. Recruit experienced professionals and volunteers to facilitate these sessions. Measure the reduction in caregiver stress levels, track attendance in support groups, and gather feedback on the effectiveness of the program.

Palliative Care Education

Launch an awareness campaign to educate the community about the benefits of palliative care. Many individuals are unaware of the options available to them when dealing with serious illness, and this project seeks to change that. Increase awareness and understanding of palliative care services, reduce stigma, and encourage earlier consideration of palliative care as part of healthcare planning. Develop informational materials, organize community seminars and workshops, and collaborate with local healthcare providers to promote the campaign. Measure the increase in the number of individuals seeking palliative care services, track attendance at awareness events, and assess changes in public perception.

Comfort Care Kits for Patients

Create and distribute comfort care kits to patients receiving palliative care. These kits can include items like soothing music, comforting blankets, journals, and other items that provide physical and emotional comfort. Improve the quality of life for patients by offering personalized comfort items that enhance their well-being. Assemble care kits with the help of volunteers, gather patient preferences and needs, and distribute these kits to patients in your care. Gather feedback from patients and their families regarding the impact of the care kits on their emotional and physical comfort.
